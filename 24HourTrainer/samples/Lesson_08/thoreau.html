Famous Quotes of Henry David Thoreau

Henry David Thoreau is best known for his books and essays.

However, many of his quotations resonate with us today. Here are just a few of his many quotes, categorized by subject:

On Living - On Books - On Government

On Living

Every man is the builder of a temple called his body.

Our life is frittered away by detail. Simplify, simplify.

For more than five years I maintained myself thus solely by the labour of my hands, and I found, that by working about six weeks in a year, I could meet all the expenses of living.

Beware of all enterprises that require new clothes.

Be not simply good - be good for something.

On Books

Before printing was discovered, a century was equal to a thousand years.

Books are the treasured wealth of the world and the fit inheritance of generations and nations.

Books are to be distinguished by the grandeur of their topics even more than by the manner in which they are treated.

Books can only reveal us to ourselves, and as often as they do us this service we lay them aside.

On Government

I heartily accept the motto, "That government is best which governs least"; and I should like to see it acted up to more rapidly and systematically.

Under a government which imprisons any unjustly, the true place for a just man is also a prison.

Disobedience is the true foundation of liberty. The obedient must be slaves.

How does it become a man to behave towards the American government today? I answer, that he cannot without disgrace be associated with it.
